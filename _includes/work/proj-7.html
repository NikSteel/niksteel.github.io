I created this cymatics rig to demonstrate the physical movement and appearance of sound waves. A layer of water rests above the surface of a large loudspeaker. When the water is excited by low frequency sounds, the sound waves become visible as ripples on the surface of the water. Different frequencies cause different wave patterns, so I used a LEAP motion sensor and Max/MSP to allow a person to control the frequency of the sound using the height of his or her hand, much like playing a theremin.
